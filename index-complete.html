THE NIL CHAOS PARENTS ARE NAVIGATING

The NIL market is booming, but families are left behind

Student-athletes today have a real, legal pathway to monetize their name, image, and likeness (NIL) through affiliate marketing, brand partnerships, digital content, and now, university revenue-sharing. But the landscape is confusing, fragmented, and filled with risk. Parents are expected to act as lawyers, agents, tax advisors, and business managers with no training, tools, or support.

What Parents Are Up Against:

Laws vary wildly by state, school, and conference

Navigating different regulations across jurisdictions

Contracts are complex, inconsistent, and often exploitative

Without legal expertise, families risk unfair deals

Most families don't understand tax, eligibility, or reporting implications

Financial and compliance risks threaten athlete futures

Unlicensed "reps" and opportunists are flooding the market

Families vulnerable to scams and bad advice

Traditional sports agencies aren't built to scale

They serve a few elite athletes — not the tens of thousands of NIL deals emerging each year at the youth, high school, and college levels.

Athletes face injury, fraud, and identity theft

Yet most young athletes have no insurance, no brand protection, and no business structure to defend future NIL earnings.

Families are unprotected, unprepared, and uninformed despite spending thousands annually on their child's athletic development.
